### Round 3: Goal Alignment Challenge
Question whether the proposed direction aligns with the product vision of being a "Historian, not stock picker" tool that helps beginners understand companies through historical context and plain-language explanations.

Does this architectural change help us be better historians, or is it merely internal housekeeping? - Organizing the service layer into domain sub-packages (charts, analysis, explanation, etc.) could actually enhance our historian positioning by making it easier to locate and maintain historical explanation components. - However, we must ensure that standardization doesn't lead to a homogenized look that loses the PPT-style charm of having different visual treatments for different types of historical information (e.g., a timeline might look different from a financial chart to signal different types of historical content).
- Designing the C170 glossary tooltip and C188 explanation card directly supports the historian mission if these components are used to provide historical context and plain-language explanations. But we must guard against them becoming tools for quick-reference definitions that encourage surface-level understanding rather than deep historical engagement.
